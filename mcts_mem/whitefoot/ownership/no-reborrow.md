- Every borrow's provenance is a singleton for its entire life (theorem T-A): borrows bind once, never rebind at control-flow merges, and `set` through a ref-mode binding writes through it rather than retargeting it.
- Uniq borrows are affine: passing one to a callee consumes it; there is no implicit reborrow, no reborrow-through-holder, and no result reborrow.
- Exclusive access flows through a call chain by linear threading — the borrow (or the owned value) passes in and comes back out — or the code restructures.
- Scattered deep writes are modeled as returned write intents: deep functions stay read-only and one shallow function applies the intents under the single exclusive borrow (the command-buffer pattern, P1).

- 2026-07-14 stored-transition correction: a borrow leaf that remains live through movement, relocation, projection, yield, or abandonment preserves its exact root, while a lawful call, replacement, or clone may end that leaf and create a new one only under the declared behavior or result-provenance relation. - 2026-07-14 hasher-reborrow correction: call-scoped reborrowing preserves the `BuildHasher S` or `Hasher H` owner, not an immutable internal leaf set. Declared behavior-effect relations alone may end or install leaves; a unique leaf moved from `S` into generated `H` cannot remain live in both, and no temporary receiver, owner field, or call frame becomes a provenance root. - 2026-07-14 shared-receiver correction: a result region may be bounded by a shared receiver even when the physical root is a receiver field, a declared external root, or static storage, and the call may still evolve unrelated receiver leaves through its declared behavior effect. Mutable projection may bounded-reborrow or transfer a unique external leaf only after the source leaf ends or enters the child reborrow; the receiver remains excluded until that child ends. A temporary receiver, address, or call frame never becomes a root merely because it bounds the result region. (sourced)
- 2026-07-14 cursor-close correction: terminal `None` does not end a source-borrowing cursor's parent authority, though an exact concrete adapter transition may retire or replace its named subcursor/epoch. Parent reuse remains rejected until cursor destruction or consuming close; proven last use may release only pure parent-borrow authority after every incompatible yielded child ends and only when no close, repair, owned-state destruction, or allocation disposition remains. If such an obligation remains, an explicit consuming close would be required, but no such production surface is selected. An owned non-lending item already moved out, including one carrying external borrow leaves, remains independent of later cursor destruction. (sourced)
